## 2. AIA A101-2017: STANDARD FORM OF AGREEMENT BETWEEN OWNER AND CONTRACTOR

### Document Overview
- **Full Title:** AIA Document A101™–2017, Standard Form of Agreement Between Owner and Contractor where the basis of payment is a Stipulated Sum
- **Purpose:** Standard form of agreement between owner and contractor for use where the basis of payment is a stipulated sum (fixed price)
- **Relationship:** Adopts by reference AIA Document A201™–2017, General Conditions of the Contract for Construction

### Key Document Structure

#### Article 1 - The Contract Documents
- Defines the Contract Documents that comprise the agreement
- Incorporates A201-2017 General Conditions by reference

#### Article 2 - The Work
- Defines the scope of work to be performed
- References drawings, specifications, and other contract documents

#### Article 3 - Date of Commencement and Substantial Completion
- § 3.1 Date of Commencement
- § 3.2 Substantial Completion date
- § 3.3 Liquidated damages provisions

#### Article 4 - Contract Sum
- § 4.1 The Contract Sum (stipulated/fixed price)
- § 4.2 Schedule of Values requirement
- § 4.3 Alternates
- § 4.4 Allowances

#### Article 5 - PAYMENTS (Key Clauses)
- **§ 5.1 Progress Payments**
  - § 5.1.1 Based on Architect's Certificate for Payment
  - § 5.1.2 Payment application process
  - § 5.1.3 Retainage provisions
  - § 5.1.4 Schedule of Values submission
  - **§ 5.1.5** "Applications for Payment shall show the percentage of completion of each portion of the Work as of the end of the period covered by the Application for Payment"
  - § 5.1.6 Reference to AIA Document A201-2017

- **§ 5.2 Final Payment**
  - Due upon Substantial Completion
  - Subject to lien waiver requirements

#### Article 6 - Completion
- § 6.1 Substantial Completion
- § 6.2 Final Completion
- § 6.3 Liquidated damages

#### Article 7 - Termination
- § 7.1 Termination for Cause
- § 7.2 Termination for Convenience (with termination fee provision)

#### Article 8 - Insurance and Bonds
- § 8.1 General Insurance Requirements
- § 8.2 Workers Compensation
- § 8.3 Property Insurance
- § 8.4 Bonds (Performance and Payment)

#### Exhibit A - Insurance and Bonds
- Detailed insurance requirements
- Bond forms and amounts

## 4. AIA G702/G703-1992: APPLICATION AND CERTIFICATE FOR PAYMENT

### Document Overview
- **G702™–1992:** Application and Certificate for Payment
- **G703™–1992:** Continuation Sheet
- **Purpose:** Provide convenient and complete forms on which the contractor can apply for payment and the architect can certify that payment is due

### G702-1992 Structure

#### Header Information
- Project name and location
- Owner name
- Contractor name
- Architect name
- Application number
- Period covered by application

#### Key Form Fields
1. **Contract Sum** (Original)
2. **Net Change by Change Orders** (Additions/Deductions)
3. **Contract Sum to Date**
4. **Total Completed and Stored to Date** (Work + Materials)
5. **Percent Complete** (%)
6. **Balance to Finish** (including Retainage)
7. **Retainage** (percentage held)
8. **Total Earned Less Retainage**
9. **Less Previous Certificates for Payment**
10. **Current Payment Due**
11. **Balance to Finish Including Retainage**

#### Certification Statement
> "The undersigned Contractor certifies that to the best of the Contractor's knowledge, information and belief the Work covered by this Application for Payment has been completed in accordance with the Contract Documents, that all amounts have been paid by the Contractor for Work for which previous Certificates for Payment were issued and payments received from the Owner, and that current payment shown herein is now due."

#### Architect's Certification
> "In accordance with the Contract Documents, based on on-site observations and the data comprising this Application for Payment, the Architect certifies to the Owner that to the best of the Architect's knowledge, information and belief the Work has progressed as indicated, the quality of the Work is in accordance with the Contract Documents, and the Contractor is entitled to payment of the AMOUNT CERTIFIED."

### G703-1992 Continuation Sheet
- Itemized breakdown of work items
- Schedule of Values reference
- Previous application amounts
- Current application amounts
- Materials presently stored
- Percent complete by line item

## 5. AIA LIEN WAIVER FORMS (G901-G904, 2022 Edition)

### Overview
AIA released new Lien Waiver and Release forms in 2022. These are "generic" forms intended for broad use, but AIA strongly recommends consulting an attorney to ensure compliance with state-specific requirements.

### The Four Types of AIA Lien Waiver Forms

#### G901™–2022: Conditional Waiver and Release on Progress Payment
- **Purpose:** Used when contractor conditionally waives rights to assert a lien or payment bond claim for Work performed through a specific date
- **Condition:** Waiver is conditioned upon receipt of payment
- **Use Case:** Progress payments, interim payments, partial payments
- **Key Feature:** Contains "Exceptions" section preserving rights to:
  - Future work
  - Unpaid retention
  - Extras
  - Pending modifications and changes
  - Disputed claims

#### G902™–2022: Unconditional Waiver and Release on Progress Payment
- **Purpose:** Used when contractor unconditionally waives rights to assert a lien or payment bond claim
- **Condition:** Waiver is effective immediately upon execution, regardless of payment receipt
- **Use Case:** When payment has already been received
- **Risk:** Waives rights even if check bounces or payment fails

#### G903™–2022: Conditional Waiver and Release on Final Payment
- **Purpose:** Used for final payment, conditionally waiving all remaining lien rights
- **Condition:** Effective only upon receipt of final payment
- **Use Case:** Final payment on project
- **Key Feature:** Covers all work through final completion

#### G904™–2022: Unconditional Waiver and Release on Final Payment
- **Purpose:** Used for final payment, unconditionally waiving all remaining lien rights
- **Condition:** Effective immediately upon execution
- **Use Case:** When final payment has been received and cleared
- **Effect:** Complete release of all lien rights on project

### Common Form Structure (All G901-G904)

#### Header Information
- Project name
- Project address/location
- Property description
- Owner name
- Contractor name
- Payment application number (if applicable)
- Effective date

#### Waiver Language Structure
> "This Conditional/Unconditional Waiver and Release on Progress/Final Payment covers a progress/final payment for Work furnished for the Project or the Property and the improvements thereon through [DATE] ('Effective Date')."

#### Exceptions Section (Conditional Waivers)
> "This Conditional Waiver and Release on Progress Payment does not cover any of the following:
> - Future Work to be performed after the Effective Date;
> - Unpaid retention, if any;
> - Extras, pending modifications and changes, and disputed claims, if any."

#### Signature Block
- Claimant/Contractor signature
- Date
- Notarization (if required by jurisdiction)

### Important Considerations

#### State-Specific Requirements
- Many states have statutory lien waiver forms that must be used
- AIA forms are generic and may not comply with all state requirements
- **AIA Recommendation:** "Consultation with an attorney is recommended to determine whether any AIA Contract Documents generic Lien Waiver and Release and/or Sworn Construction Statement form will work for your needs."

#### Related Documents
- **G706™–1994:** Contractor's Affidavit of Payment of Debts and Claims
- **G706A™–1994:** Contractor's Affidavit of Release of Liens
- **G707™–1994:** Consent of Surety to Final Payment

## 6. WHAT MAKES A DOCUMENT "OFFICIAL AIA"

### Authentication Markers

1. **Copyright Notice**
   - "Copyright © [Year] by The American Institute of Architects. All rights reserved."

2. **Trademark Symbols**
   - AIA® (registered trademark)
   - Document numbers with ™ or ® (e.g., A101™–2017)

3. **Official Source**
   - Purchased from aia.org, aiacontracts.com, or authorized AIA Document Store
   - Downloaded from official AIA member resources

4. **Document Format**
   - Standard AIA formatting and numbering
   - Official AIA logos and branding
   - Consistent section numbering across document families

5. **Warning Language**
   - "WARNING: This AIA® Document is protected by U.S. Copyright Law and International Treaties."
   - "Unauthorized reproduction or distribution of this AIA® Document..."

### Document Families
AIA documents are organized into series:
- **A-Series:** Owner-Contractor agreements
- **B-Series:** Owner-Architect agreements
- **C-Series:** Other agreements (consultants, etc.)
- **D-Series:** Miscellaneous documents
- **E-Series:** Exhibits
- **G-Series:** Contract administration forms (G702, G703, G901-G904)
