# SBA Loan Guide for Contractors
## Practical Steps to Secure Funding

---

## QUICK DECISION TREE

**Need money fast (under 30 days)?** → SBA Express or online lender  
**Buying real estate/equipment?** → SBA 504  
**General working capital?** → SBA 7(a)  
**Just starting out?** → SBA Microloan  
**Bad credit but good business?** → Community bank or credit union

---

## THE APPLICATION PROCESS

### Week 1: Preparation
- Pull credit reports (fix errors first)
- Gather all documents
- Draft business plan
- Calculate exact loan amount needed

### Week 2: Lender Shopping
- Contact 3-5 SBA-approved lenders
- Compare rates and terms
- Ask about their construction industry experience

### Week 3-4: Submission
- Complete SBA Form 1919 (borrower info)
- Complete SBA Form 413 (personal financial statement)
- Submit with all attachments

### Week 5-8: Underwriting
- Respond to lender requests quickly
- Provide additional docs as needed
- Be patient but follow up weekly

### Week 9-10: Closing
- Review loan terms carefully
- Sign documents
- Receive funds

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get an SBA loan with bad credit?**  
A: Possible but harder. Minimum 600, ideally 650+. Explain negative marks. Consider community banks or adding a co-signer.

**Q: How much can I actually borrow?**  
A: Generally 10-15% of annual revenue for working capital. Real estate loans based on collateral value. New businesses limited to $50K-100K.

**Q: Do I need collateral?**  
A: Yes for loans over $25K. Real estate, equipment, accounts receivable all count. Personal assets may be required.

**Q: Can I use the loan to buy a truck?**  
A: Yes, if it's for business use. 504 loan is best for equipment over $150K. 7(a) works for smaller purchases.

**Q: What if I'm a new contractor (under 2 years)?**  
A: Microloan or Express loan. Strong business plan and industry experience elsewhere helps. Expect lower amounts.

**Q: Can I pay off the loan early?**  
A: Usually yes, but check prepayment penalties. Some SBA loans have penalties if paid off in first 3 years.

**Q: What's the interest rate?**  
A: Prime + 2.25-4.75% depending on loan type and amount. Currently around 8-12% total.

**Q: Do I need a business plan?**  
A: Yes, always. Even for small loans. 2-5 pages covering business, market, financials, and use of funds.

**Q: Can I apply to multiple lenders at once?**  
A: Yes, but disclose it. Multiple credit pulls within 14 days count as one inquiry.

**Q: What kills applications most often?**  
A: Incomplete docs, unrealistic projections, recent bankruptcy, unpaid taxes, criminal history.

## RED FLAGS TO AVOID

✗ Waiting until you're desperate (plan 2-3 months ahead)  
✗ Hiding existing debt or legal issues  
✗ Inflating revenue projections  
✗ Applying without a clear use for funds  
✗ Ignoring your credit report errors  
✗ Choosing a lender with no construction experience  
✗ Missing tax filings or having unfiled returns  
✗ Recent bankruptcy (wait 2-3 years)

## LENDER SELECTION TIPS

**Ask these questions:**
1. How many construction loans did you fund last year?
2. What's your average time to close?
3. Do you service the loan or sell it?
4. What are your upfront fees?
5. Who will be my main contact during the process?

**Prefer lenders who:**
- Understand construction industry cycles
- Offer local decision-making
- Have dedicated SBA departments
- Can close in under 60 days

---

## USE OF FUNDS (What You Can/Cannot Do)

**✓ ALLOWED:**
- Working capital
- Equipment purchases
- Real estate acquisition/construction
- Refinancing existing debt
- Inventory and materials
- Business acquisition

**✗ NOT ALLOWED:**
- Paying off delinquent taxes
- Illegal activities
- Speculative investments
- Owner distributions/dividends
- Personal expenses

---

## ALTERNATIVES IF DENIED

1. **Community banks** - More flexible than big banks
2. **Credit unions** - Member-focused, competitive rates
3. **Equipment financing** - Easier approval for specific purchases
4. **Invoice factoring** - Use receivables for cash flow
5. **Business line of credit** - Revolving access to funds
6. **Private investors** - Equity or debt from individuals
7. **CDFI lenders** - Community Development Financial Institutions

## QUICK REFERENCE: SBA FORMS YOU'LL NEED

- **SBA Form 1919** - Borrower Information Form
- **SBA Form 413** - Personal Financial Statement  
- **SBA Form 159** - Fee Disclosure (if using broker)
- **SBA Form 912** - Statement of Personal History
- **SBA Form 1368** - Application for Business Loan

Most lenders use their own online systems now, but these are the underlying requirements.

## AFTER YOU GET THE LOAN

**Do:**
- Use funds exactly as stated in application
- Keep detailed records
- Make payments on time
- Communicate with lender if problems arise
- Track how loan impacts your business

**Don't:**
- Use funds for unauthorized purposes
- Miss payments without contacting lender
- Take on additional debt without informing lender
- Ignore covenants or reporting requirements
