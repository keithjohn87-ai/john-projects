# ContractorPro Big League Outreach Package

## For $100-200M Contractors (Advisors, Not Customers)

---

## Cover Letter / Email Template

**Subject:** Quick favor — testing something, need your eyes on it

Hey [Name],

Building a business system for smaller contractors (the $1-10M guys who don't have what you have).

Won't take more than 10 minutes — just need to know if I'm solving the right problems.

**What's in the attached package:**
- Document templates (liens, notices, contracts)
- Job costing and project tracking spreadsheets  
- 50-state lien law database
- Subcontractor and COI tracking tools

**The question:** If you were advising a $5M contractor who has nothing, would you tell them to build this or buy something else?

No pitch, no follow-up. Just need the reality check from someone who's been there.

Thanks,
[Your name]

---

## What You're Really After

**Not:** Them using the product (they won't)
**Yes:** Their validation + network access

**The Real Questions (in your head, not the email):**
1. Would they recommend this to their subcontractors?
2. Do their subs struggle with these problems?
3. Would they intro you to their vendor network?
4. Would they white-label this for their own use?

---

## Follow-Up Strategy (If They Respond)

**Positive response:**
> "Glad it doesn't completely miss the mark. Quick follow-up — do your subs struggle with this stuff? Wondering if there's a way to get it in front of them."

**Negative response (even better):**
> "Appreciate the honesty. What would you tell a $5M contractor to do instead?"

**No response (2 weeks):**
> "No worries if you didn't get a chance to look. If you know any contractors in the $1-10M range who might benefit from this, happy to send it their way."

---

## The Ask (If Conversation Goes Well)

**Option 1: Subcontractor Network**
> "Would you be open to me mentioning you suggested I reach out? Or better — any chance you'd intro me to a couple of your subs who might need this?"

**Option 2: Vendor/Partner Introduction**
> "Do you work with any industry associations or buying groups that serve smaller contractors? Might be a fit there."

**Option 3: White Label (Long Shot)**
> "If this works for smaller guys, would [Company] ever want to offer something like this as a value-add to your network?"

---

## What Success Looks Like

**Best case:** They intro you to 3 subcontractors who become customers
**Good case:** They validate the problem exists at smaller levels
**Decent case:** They tell you what's wrong so you can fix it
**Worst case:** They ignore you (you're no worse off)

---

## The Mindset

These guys are:
- ✅ Decision-makers (no gatekeepers for a 10-minute favor)
- ✅ Connected (they know everyone)
- ✅ Validators (their opinion carries weight)
- ❌ Not your customers (don't try to sell them)

**Your job:** Get 10 minutes of their brain. Turn that into intros, validation, or product fixes.

**Their job:** Give advice (which they love doing).

Everyone wins.

---

## The Package to Send

**File:** `business-system-199.zip` (the full package)

**Why the full package:** Shows you're serious. They can see everything. Nothing hidden.

**Include:**
- All templates and trackers
- README explaining what each file does
- States.json (shows the 50-state scope)

**Don't include:**
- Pricing (irrelevant to them)
- Sales pitch (kills the favor)
- Demo request (they don't need it)

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

**If they say:**
- "We built all this internally" → Ask: "Would you recommend your approach to a $5M contractor?"
- "Our subs use [Big Software Company]" → Ask: "What about the subs who can't afford that?"
- "Seems basic" → Ask: "What am I missing that smaller guys actually need?"

**They're testing you.** Pass = deeper conversation. Fail = polite dismissal.

---

## The End Game

You're not building a product for $100M contractors.

You're using their expertise to build a product for the thousands of contractors who wish they were $100M contractors.

That's the market. These guys are just the advisors.

---

**Send. Listen. Adapt.**
