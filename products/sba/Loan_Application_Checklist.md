# SBA Loan Application Checklist for Contractors

## Overview
This comprehensive checklist will help you prepare for and complete your SBA loan application. Use this guide to ensure you have all required documentation before starting the application process.

---

## Pre-Application Preparation

### Business Information
- [ ] Business legal name and DBA (if applicable)
- [ ] Business entity type (LLC, Corp, Partnership, Sole Prop)
- [ ] Date business established
- [ ] Federal Tax ID (EIN)
- [ ] State business registration number
- [ ] Contractor license number(s)
- [ ] NAICS code (typically 238 for specialty trade contractors)

### Personal Information (All owners 20%+)
- [ ] Full legal names
- [ ] Social Security Numbers
- [ ] Date of birth
- [ ] Citizenship status
- [ ] Home addresses (2+ years)
- [ ] Percentage of ownership
- [ ] Resume/CV for each owner

---

## Required Financial Documents

### Business Financials
- [ ] **Profit & Loss Statements** (last 3 years + YTD)
- [ ] **Balance Sheets** (last 3 years + current)
- [ ] **Cash Flow Statements** (last 3 years + projections)
- [ ] **Business Tax Returns** (last 3 years)
  - Form 1120 (Corporations)
  - Form 1120S (S-Corps)
  - Form 1065 (Partnerships)
  - Schedule C (Sole Props)
- [ ] **Accounts Receivable Aging Report**
- [ ] **Accounts Payable Aging Report**
- [ ] **Debt Schedule** (all business debts)
- [ ] **Business Bank Statements** (last 12 months)

### Personal Financials (All owners 20%+)
- [ ] **Personal Tax Returns** (last 3 years)
- [ ] **Personal Financial Statement** (SBA Form 413)
- [ ] **Personal Bank Statements** (last 3 months)
- [ ] **List of Personal Assets**
  - Real estate
  - Vehicles
  - Investment accounts
  - Retirement accounts
  - Life insurance cash value
- [ ] **List of Personal Liabilities**
  - Mortgages
  - Vehicle loans
  - Credit cards
  - Student loans
  - Other personal debts

## Tips for Success

### Before Applying
1. **Check your credit** - Review personal and business credit reports
2. **Organize documents** - Have everything ready before starting
3. **Know your numbers** - Be prepared to explain your financials
4. **Prepare your story** - Be ready to explain your business and loan need

### During the Process
1. **Respond quickly** - Delays in providing documents slow approval
2. **Be honest** - Disclose all relevant information upfront
3. **Ask questions** - Understand the terms and requirements
4. **Stay organized** - Keep copies of everything submitted

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- [ ] Incomplete application
- [ ] Missing signatures
- [ ] Inconsistent information
- [ ] Outdated financial statements
- [ ] Unrealistic projections
- [ ] Insufficient collateral explanation
- [ ] Poor credit explanation (if applicable)

## SBA Loan Programs for Contractors

### 7(a) Loan Program
- **Amount**: Up to $5 million
- **Use**: Working capital, equipment, real estate, business acquisition
- **Term**: Up to 25 years (real estate), 10 years (equipment), 7 years (working capital)
- **Best for**: General business purposes

### 504 Loan Program
- **Amount**: Up to $5.5 million
- **Use**: Major fixed assets (real estate, equipment)
- **Term**: 10, 20, or 25 years
- **Best for**: Real estate purchase or major equipment

### Microloan Program
- **Amount**: Up to $50,000
- **Use**: Working capital, inventory, supplies, equipment
- **Term**: Up to 6 years
- **Best for**: Smaller needs, startups

### SBA Express
- **Amount**: Up to $500,000
- **Use**: Working capital, equipment
- **Term**: Up to 10 years
- **Best for**: Faster approval (36 hours)
