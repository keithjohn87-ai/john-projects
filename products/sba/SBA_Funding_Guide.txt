1. SBA LOAN TYPES OVERVIEW

The SBA doesn't lend money directly (except for Disaster Loans). Instead, they guarantee loans made by approved lenders.

BEST FOR CONTRACTORS:
→ 7(a) Loans: Working capital, equipment, refinancing
→ 504 Loans: Real estate, major equipment
→ Microloans: Smaller amounts, startup costs

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

2. 7(a) LOAN PROGRAM

WHAT IT'S FOR:
• Working capital
• Equipment purchases
• Inventory
• Refinancing existing debt
• Business acquisition

LOAN AMOUNTS: Up to $5 million
TERMS: Up to 25 years for real estate, 10 years for equipment
INTEREST RATES: Prime + 2.25% to 4.75%

REQUIREMENTS:
• Good credit (680+ preferred)
• 2+ years in business
• Demonstrated cash flow
• Collateral (may be required)
• Personal guarantee

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3. 504 LOAN PROGRAM

WHAT IT'S FOR:
• Commercial real estate purchase/construction
• Major equipment (10+ year life)
• Renovation/improvements

STRUCTURE:
• 50% from bank
• 40% from CDC (SBA-backed)
• 10% from borrower (down payment)

LOAN AMOUNTS: Up to $5.5 million
TERMS: 10, 20, or 25 years
INTEREST RATES: Fixed, below-market rates

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

4. MICROLOAN PROGRAM

WHAT IT'S FOR:
• Startup costs
• Working capital
• Small equipment
• Inventory

LOAN AMOUNTS: Up to $50,000
TERMS: Up to 6 years
INTEREST RATES: 8% to 13%

BEST FOR: New businesses, smaller needs

5. DOCUMENT CHECKLISTS

ALL LOANS REQUIRE:
□ Business tax returns (2-3 years)
□ Personal tax returns (2-3 years)
□ Year-to-date financial statements
□ Business plan
□ Personal financial statement
□ Debt schedule
□ Business licenses
□ Articles of incorporation

CONTRACTOR-SPECIFIC:
□ Contractor license
□ Bond information
□ Insurance certificates
□ Equipment list
□ Major contracts/projects
□ Customer references
□ Bank statements (6-12 months)

7(a) ADDITIONAL:
□ Cash flow projections (2 years)
□ Use of funds statement
□ Resumes of key management

504 ADDITIONAL:
□ Real estate appraisal
□ Environmental assessment
□ Project cost breakdown
□ Job creation estimates

6. APPLICATION WALKTHROUGH

STEP 1: PREPARATION (2-4 weeks)
• Gather all documents
• Review credit reports
• Prepare business plan
• Calculate funding needs

STEP 2: LENDER SELECTION
• Find SBA Preferred Lender
• Compare rates and terms
• Check contractor experience

STEP 3: APPLICATION
• Complete SBA forms
• Submit to lender
• Pay application fee

STEP 4: UNDERWRITING (30-60 days)
• Lender review
• SBA review (if needed)
• Additional documentation
• Site visit (possible)

STEP 5: APPROVAL & CLOSING
• Commitment letter
• Final documentation
• Closing
• Funding

7. LENDER MATCHING STRATEGIES

FIND SBA LENDERS:
• SBA.gov lender search
• Local banks with SBA departments
• Credit unions
• Online SBA lenders

WHAT TO ASK:
• How many contractor loans have you done?
• What's your average approval time?
• What are your rates and fees?
• Do you service the loan or sell it?

RED FLAGS:
• No contractor experience
• Vague about timeline
• High upfront fees
• Pressure tactics

8. GRANT OPPORTUNITIES

FEDERAL:
• SBIR/STTR programs (R&D focused)
• USDA Rural Business Grants

STATE/LOCAL:
• Economic development grants
• Workforce training grants
• Energy efficiency grants

INDUSTRY-SPECIFIC:
• Green building incentives
• Veteran-owned business grants
• Minority-owned business grants

TIPS:
• Grants are competitive
• Often require matching funds
• Reporting requirements strict
• Combine with loans for leverage

9. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

❌ Insufficient documentation
❌ Unrealistic projections
❌ Poor credit preparation
❌ Wrong loan type for need
❌ Rushing the process
❌ Not comparing lenders
❌ Ignoring personal guarantee
❌ Underestimating timeline

DO THIS INSTEAD:
✓ Prepare thoroughly
✓ Be conservative in projections
✓ Clean up credit issues first
✓ Match loan type to use of funds
✓ Start 3-6 months before need
✓ Shop multiple lenders
✓ Understand all terms
✓ Build in buffer time
