## 1. UNDERSTANDING SBA LOAN TYPES

### SBA 7(a) Loan
- **Amount:** Up to $5 million
- **Use:** Working capital, equipment, real estate, refinancing
- **Terms:** 10-25 years
- **Best for:** General business needs, expansion

### SBA 504 Loan
- **Amount:** Up to $5.5 million
- **Use:** Major fixed assets (real estate, heavy equipment)
- **Terms:** 10-20 years
- **Best for:** Property purchase, large equipment

### SBA Microloan
- **Amount:** Up to $50,000
- **Use:** Working capital, inventory, supplies
- **Terms:** Up to 6 years
- **Best for:** Small projects, startups

### SBA Express
- **Amount:** Up to $500,000
- **Use:** Fast working capital
- **Terms:** Up to 10 years
- **Best for:** Quick funding needs

## 4. APPLICATION WALKTHROUGH

### Step 1: Pre-Qualification (Week 1)
- Check credit score
- Gather preliminary documents
- Calculate debt service coverage ratio
- Identify collateral

### Step 2: Lender Selection (Week 1-2)
- Research SBA Preferred Lenders in your area
- Compare rates and terms
- Schedule consultations (3-5 lenders)

### Step 3: Application Submission (Week 2-3)
- Complete SBA Form 1919 (Borrower Information)
- Submit all documentation
- Pay application fee

### Step 4: Underwriting (Week 4-8)
- Respond to lender questions promptly
- Provide additional docs as requested
- Site visit may be scheduled

### Step 5: Approval & Closing (Week 8-10)
- Review loan terms carefully
- Sign closing documents
- Funds disbursed

## 5. LENDER MATCHING STRATEGIES

### Top SBA Lenders for Construction
1. **Live Oak Bank** - Specializes in construction
2. **Newtek** - High approval rates for contractors
3. **Wells Fargo** - Large network, competitive rates
4. **Chase** - Relationship banking benefits
5. **Local/community banks** - Personalized service

### Questions to Ask Lenders
- How many construction loans have you closed?
- What's your average approval time?
- What are your rates and fees?
- Do you require personal guarantee?
- What's your relationship with SBA?

### Red Flags
- Lender has no construction experience
- Pressure to sign quickly
- Unclear fee structure
- Poor communication

## 7. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### Documentation Mistakes
- ❌ Incomplete financial statements
- ❌ Missing tax returns
- ❌ No business plan
- ❌ Outdated contractor license

### Strategic Mistakes
- ❌ Applying to only one lender
- ❌ Not checking credit first
- ❌ Underestimating time needed
- ❌ Not having collateral ready

### Communication Mistakes
- ❌ Slow response to lender questions
- ❌ Not disclosing all debts
- ❌ Changing business structure during application
