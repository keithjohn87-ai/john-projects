## Regional & Community Banks

### Advantages of Local Banks:
- Personal relationship with decision makers
- Understanding of local market
- More flexible on exceptions
- Faster for simple loans
- Community investment focus

### How to Find Good Local SBA Lenders:

**1. SBA Lender Search Tool:**
- Visit: sba.gov/lendersearch
- Enter your zip code
- Filter by "SBA 7(a)" or "SBA 504"
- Look for "Preferred Lenders" (PLP)

**2. Local SBA District Office:**
- Find yours at: sba.gov/local-assistance
- Call and ask for lender referrals
- Ask which lenders work with contractors

**3. Ask Other Contractors:**
- Who did they use?
- Would they recommend them?
- What was the experience like?

**4. Check with Professional Associations:**
- Associated General Contractors (AGC)
- National Association of Home Builders (NAHB)
- Local contractor associations

## Questions to Ask Every Lender

### Experience & Track Record
1. How many SBA loans did you close last year?
2. How many were for construction contractors?
3. What was your average loan size for contractors?
4. What percentage of your SBA applications get approved?
5. How long have you been doing SBA loans?

### Process & Timeline
6. What's your typical timeline from application to closing?
7. Who will be my primary contact?
8. How often will I get updates?
9. What can delay the process?
10. Do you handle the SBA submission or do I need to?

### Terms & Requirements
11. What are your current interest rates?
12. What fees do you charge?
13. What collateral do you require?
14. Do you require a personal guarantee?
15. What's your minimum credit score?
16. What's your minimum DSCR requirement?

### Relationship & Support
17. What happens if I have trouble making payments?
18. Can I pay off the loan early? Any prepayment penalties?
19. Do you require me to bank with you?
20. What other services do you offer contractors?

## Red Flags: Lenders to Avoid

**🚩 Major Warning Signs:**

1. **Can't provide references** from other contractors
2. **Pressure to commit immediately** without review
3. **Vague about fees** or total costs
4. **Poor communication** during initial meetings
5. **No construction loan experience**
6. **Promises guaranteed approval** (no one can guarantee)
7. **Requires upfront payment** before approval
8. **Unwilling to explain** terms or process
9. **No physical office** or verifiable address
10. **Asks you to lie** or misrepresent information

**🚩 Moderate Concerns:**

1. Significantly higher rates than competitors
2. Excessive fees
3. Very small SBA loan volume
4. Poor online reviews
5. High turnover in loan officers

## SBA Preferred Lender Program (PLP)

**What is PLP?**
- Lenders authorized by SBA to make loans without prior SBA review
- Faster approval process
- More flexible on some requirements
- Experienced with SBA procedures

**Benefits of Working with PLP Lenders:**
- Approval in days, not weeks
- More control over process
- Better for time-sensitive deals
- Often more construction experience

**How to Identify PLP Lenders:**
- Ask: "Are you a Preferred Lender?"
- SBA lender search tool shows PLP status
- Most top lenders are PLP

## Credit Union SBA Lenders

**Advantages:**
- Lower rates (member-owned)
- Personalized service
- Community focus
- May have construction expertise

**Disadvantages:**
- Limited geographic reach
- Smaller loan amounts
- Fewer locations
- May lack sophisticated SBA expertise

**Examples:**
- Navy Federal Credit Union (veterans/military)
- Local construction credit unions
- Regional credit unions with SBA programs

## Online vs. Traditional Lenders

| Factor | Online Lenders | Traditional Banks |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| Speed | Faster | Slower |
| Convenience | 100% digital | In-person required |
| Rates | Competitive | Often lower |
| Relationship | Transactional | Long-term |
| Flexibility | Less | More |
| Construction Expertise | Varies | Often better |
| Maximum Loan | $5M typical | $5M+ possible |

**Recommendation:** For construction contractors, traditional lenders with construction expertise often provide better value despite longer timelines.
