# 50-State Mechanics Lien Law Reference Guide

## Overview
This comprehensive guide provides essential information about mechanics lien laws across all 50 states. Use this reference to understand filing deadlines, notice requirements, and enforcement procedures in your state.

---

## ALABAMA
- **Filing Deadline**: 6 months from last furnishing labor/materials
- **Preliminary Notice**: Not required for direct contractors
- **Lien Period**: 12 months to enforce (sue)
- **Who Can File**: Contractors, subcontractors, suppliers, laborers
- **Key Notes**: Must file statement of lien with probate court

## ALASKA
- **Filing Deadline**: 90 days from last furnishing
- **Preliminary Notice**: Required within 15 days of first furnishing
- **Lien Period**: 6 months to enforce
- **Who Can File**: All construction parties
- **Key Notes**: Notice must be recorded to be valid

## ARIZONA
- **Filing Deadline**: 120 days from completion (or 60 days from notice of completion)
- **Preliminary Notice**: Required within 20 days of first furnishing
- **Lien Period**: 6 months to enforce
- **Who Can File**: Licensed contractors, subcontractors, suppliers
- **Key Notes**: 20-day preliminary notice is critical

## ARKANSAS
- **Filing Deadline**: 120 days from last furnishing
- **Preliminary Notice**: Not required
- **Lien Period**: 15 months to enforce
- **Who Can File**: Contractors, subcontractors, suppliers
- **Key Notes**: Must file affidavit of indebtedness

## CALIFORNIA

- **Preliminary Notice**: Required within 20 days of first furnishing
- **Lien Period**: 90 days to enforce
- **Who Can File**: All construction parties
- **Key Notes**: Strict 20-day notice rule; licensed contractors only

## COLORADO
