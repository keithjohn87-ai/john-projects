## Foundation

### Define Your Brand

#### Brand Identity
- **Mission Statement:** Why does your company exist?
- **Vision Statement:** Where are you going?
- **Core Values:** What principles guide your work?
- **Brand Personality:** How do you want to be perceived?

#### Unique Selling Proposition (USP)
What makes you different from every other contractor?

Examples:
- "We guarantee project completion dates or we pay you $100/day"
- "The only contractor in [City] specializing in historic home restoration"
- "Family-owned for 3 generations with 500+ 5-star reviews"

#### Target Customer Avatar
Create a detailed profile of your ideal customer:
- Demographics (age, income, location)
- Psychographics (values, interests, lifestyle)
- Pain points and challenges
- Buying behavior and decision process
- Where they spend time online and offline

### Competitive Positioning

#### Market Analysis
| Competitor | Their Strength | Their Weakness | Your Opportunity |
|------------|----------------|----------------|------------------|
| Big Box Contractor | Brand recognition | Impersonal service | Local, personalized approach |
| Cheap Competitor | Low prices | Poor quality | Quality at fair prices |
| Boutique Firm | High-end work | Limited availability | Faster turnaround |

#### Price Positioning
- **Premium:** Top 10% pricing, luxury market
- **Value:** Mid-market, best value proposition
- **Budget:** Competitive pricing, volume-focused

Choose ONE and own it.

## Digital Marketing

### Website Optimization

#### Essential Pages
1. **Homepage**
   - Clear headline stating what you do
   - Trust indicators (reviews, licenses, years in business)
   - Strong call-to-action (CTA)
   - Professional project photos

2. **Services Pages**
   - One page per service type
   - Detailed descriptions
   - Process explanation
   - FAQs
   - Related project photos

3. **Portfolio/Gallery**
   - Before/after photos
   - Project details and scope
   - Client testimonials
   - Filter by project type

4. **About Page**
   - Company story
   - Team photos and bios
   - Licenses and certifications
   - Community involvement

5. **Contact Page**
   - Multiple contact methods
   - Simple contact form
   - Map and directions
   - Hours of operation

#### Technical SEO
- Mobile-responsive design
- Fast loading speed (under 3 seconds)
- SSL certificate (HTTPS)
- Schema markup for local business
- XML sitemap
- Google Search Console setup

#### Conversion Optimization
- Click-to-call phone numbers
- Online booking/estimate requests
- Live chat (if manageable)
- Exit-intent popups with offers
- Trust badges and certifications

### Local SEO

#### Google Business Profile
- Complete all sections 100%
- Add 10+ photos monthly
- Post weekly updates
- Respond to all reviews within 24 hours
- Use Google Posts for promotions
- Enable messaging
- Add Q&A section
- Set service areas

#### Local Citations
Ensure consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) on:
- Yelp
- Angi (Angie's List)
- HomeAdvisor
- Houzz
- Better Business Bureau
- Local chamber of commerce
- Industry-specific directories

#### Review Generation Strategy
**The 3-Step Process:**
1. **Timing:** Ask when customer is happiest (project completion)
2. **Method:** Make it easy - send direct review link via text/email
3. **Follow-up:** One gentle reminder if needed

**Review Response Templates:**
- Positive: Thank them specifically, mention their project
- Negative: Acknowledge, apologize, offer to make it right, take offline

### Content Marketing

#### Blog Topics That Generate Leads
- "How much does [service] cost in [city]?"
- "5 things to know before hiring a [trade] contractor"
- "[Service] vs [Alternative]: Which is right for you?"
- "How to prepare for your [service] project"
- "Common [service] mistakes and how to avoid them"

#### Video Content Ideas
- Project time-lapses
- Customer testimonial interviews
- "Day in the life" of your team
- How-to tips (simple DIY)
- Before/after reveals
- Equipment/process explanations

#### Social Media Strategy

**Facebook:**
- Post frequency: 3-5x per week
- Content mix: 40% projects, 30% educational, 20% personal/behind-scenes, 10% promotional
- Use Facebook Groups for community engagement
- Run targeted local ads

**Instagram:**
- Post frequency: Daily Stories, 3-4 feed posts per week
- Focus on visuals: Before/after, process shots, team photos
- Use location tags and relevant hashtags
- Engage with local accounts

**LinkedIn:**
- Post frequency: 2-3x per week
- Content: Industry insights, business growth, professional achievements
- Connect with commercial prospects, property managers

**YouTube:**
- Post frequency: Weekly
- Content: Project showcases, how-to guides, customer stories
- Optimize titles for search
- Create playlists by service type

### Paid Advertising

#### Google Ads Strategy

**Campaign Structure:**
1. **Brand Campaign** (your company name)
2. **Service Campaigns** (one per service)
3. **Competitor Campaign** (optional)
4. **Remarketing Campaign**

**High-Intent Keywords:**
- "[service] contractor [city]"
- "[service] company near me"
- "best [service] contractor [city]"
- "[service] estimate [city]"
- "hire [trade] [city]"

**Ad Copy Framework:**
- Headline 1: Service + Location
- Headline 2: Key benefit or offer
- Headline 3: Trust indicator
- Description: Expand on benefits + CTA

**Budget Guidelines:**
- Start with $500-1,000/month
- Focus on 2-3 core services initially
- Adjust based on cost per lead

#### Facebook/Instagram Ads

**Audience Targeting:**
- Homeowners in your service area
- Age 35-65 (typical decision-makers)
- Income level matching your target market
- Interests: Home improvement, HGTV, real estate
- Behaviors: Recently moved, engaged shoppers

**Ad Types That Work:**
- Before/after photo carousels
- Video testimonials
- Lead generation forms (name, email, phone, project type)
- Special offers/discounts

**Retargeting:**
- Website visitors (past 30 days)
- Video viewers (50%+ watched)
- Page engagers (past 90 days)

## Traditional Marketing

### Direct Mail

**Postcard Campaigns:**
- Target: Specific neighborhoods or recent home buyers
- Frequency: Monthly or bi-monthly
- Size: Oversized postcards (6x11) for visibility
- Design: Professional photo, clear headline, strong offer

**Magazine Inserts:**
- Local home & garden magazines
- Seasonal timing (spring for outdoor, fall for indoor)

### Networking & Partnerships

**Strategic Referral Partners:**
- Real estate agents
- Property managers
- Interior designers
- Architects
- Insurance agents
- Other non-competing contractors

**Networking Groups:**
- BNI (Business Network International)
- Local chamber of commerce
- Home builders association
- NARI (National Association of the Remodeling Industry)

### Community Marketing

**Sponsorships:**
- Little league teams
- Local charity events
- School programs
- Community festivals

**Educational Workshops:**
- Host "How to Hire a Contractor" seminars
- Partner with home improvement stores
- Offer free consultations at community events

### Vehicle Marketing

**Truck/Trailer Wraps:**
- Professional design with clear branding
- Phone number and website prominent
- Before/after photos if space allows
- Consistent across entire fleet

**Yard Signs:**
- Place at every active job site
- Include phone number and website
- Remove promptly after project completion
- Use double-sided for visibility

## Sales Process

### Lead Management

**Lead Sources Tracking:**
Track every lead by source to know what's working:
- Website form
- Phone call
- Google Business Profile
- Referral
- Paid ad (which platform)
- Social media
- Walk-in/drive-by

**Response Time Standards:**
- Phone calls: Answer live or return within 15 minutes
- Form submissions: Respond within 1 hour during business hours
- After-hours inquiries: First thing next business day

**Lead Qualification Questions:**
1. What type of project are you considering?
2. What's your timeline?
3. Have you worked with a contractor before?
4. What's your budget range?
5. Are you the decision-maker?
6. What prompted you to start this project now?

### The Sales Consultation

**Pre-Meeting:**
- Research the property (Google Maps, Zillow)
- Prepare portfolio examples of similar work
- Review any photos/info they sent
- Confirm appointment 24 hours ahead

**During the Meeting:**
1. **Build Rapport** (5-10 min)
   - Tour the space together
   - Ask about their vision and goals
   - Listen more than talk

2. **Discover Needs** (10-15 min)
   - What's driving this project?
   - What does success look like?
   - What concerns do they have?
   - What's their decision process?

3. **Present Solutions** (15-20 min)
   - Show relevant portfolio work
   - Explain your process
   - Address their specific concerns
   - Discuss timeline

4. **Investment Discussion** (10 min)
   - Present budget range if asked
   - Explain pricing factors
   - Discuss payment terms

5. **Next Steps** (5 min)
   - Schedule follow-up
   - Explain proposal timeline
   - Leave behind materials

**The Proposal:**
- Send within 48 hours of consultation
- Include detailed scope of work
- Provide 2-3 options (good/better/best)
- Include timeline and payment schedule
- Add testimonials and warranty info
- Make it easy to say yes (electronic signature)

**Follow-Up Sequence:**
- Day 2: Check they received proposal
- Day 5: Answer any questions
- Day 10: Address concerns, offer references
- Day 14: Final check-in, create urgency if appropriate

## Customer Retention

### During the Project

**Communication Schedule:**
- Daily updates during active work
- Weekly formal progress reports
- Immediate notification of any issues or changes
- Pre-completion walkthrough

### Project Completion

**The Handoff:**
- Final walkthrough with customer
- Punch list completion
- Warranty documentation
- Care and maintenance instructions
- Request for review (with direct link)

### Post-Project Follow-Up

**30-Day Check-In:**
- Call to ensure satisfaction
- Address any minor issues
- Ask for referral

**Annual Touchpoints:**
- Birthday card to homeowner
- Seasonal maintenance reminders
- Holiday card with company update
- Newsletter with tips and company news

### Referral Program

**Structure:**
- $250 credit or gift card for referred projects over $5,000
- $500 for projects over $10,000
- Thank you gift for any referral (regardless of outcome)

**Promotion:**
- Mention at project completion
- Include in follow-up emails
- Add to email signature
- Create referral cards customers can hand out

## Measurement & Analytics

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

**Lead Generation:**
- Total leads per month
- Leads by source
- Cost per lead by channel
- Website conversion rate

**Sales Metrics:**
- Lead-to-appointment rate
- Appointment-to-proposal rate
- Proposal-to-sale rate
- Average project value
- Sales cycle length

**Marketing ROI:**
- Revenue by lead source
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Lifetime customer value (LTV)
- LTV:CAC ratio (aim for 3:1 or higher)

**Online Metrics:**
- Website traffic
- Organic search rankings
- Google Business Profile views/actions
- Social media engagement rates
- Email open/click rates

### Monthly Reporting
Create a simple dashboard tracking:
1. Leads generated (by source)
2. Appointments set
3. Proposals sent
4. Contracts signed
5. Revenue booked
6. Marketing spend
7. Cost per lead
8. Cost per acquisition

### Quarterly Review
- What's working? Do more of it.
- What's not working? Fix or eliminate.
- What new opportunities exist?
- What threats should we address?
- Budget reallocation based on performance
