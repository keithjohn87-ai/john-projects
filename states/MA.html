Massachusetts Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Massachusetts.

📋 Licensing Requirements

Massachusetts Division of Professional Licensure

Construction Supervisor License (CSL) required

Visit CSL Website →

Key Requirements:

  • CSL Required: For projects requiring building permits
  • License Types: Unrestricted, Restricted, and Specialty
  • Experience: 3+ years of building experience
  • Exam: Must pass state exam
  • Insurance: Workers' comp required with employees

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Notice of Contract: Record within 90 days of last furnishing

Statement of Account: File by earliest of: 90 days after Notice of Substantial Completion, 120 days after Notice of Termination, or 120 days after last furnishing

Foreclosure Period: Within 90 days of filing Statement of Account

⚠️ Important: Massachusetts has complex lien requirements. Record Notice of Contract first, then follow with Statement of Account by the appropriate deadline.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 6.25% state rate

Local Rates: No local sales tax in Massachusetts

Contractor Tax Notes: Contractors pay sales tax on materials. Construction services are generally exempt from sales tax.
