Colorado Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Colorado.

📋 Licensing Requirements

Colorado Department of Regulatory Agencies

Visit DORA Website →

Key Requirements:

  • State License: No statewide general contractor license required
  • Local Licensing: Required by most cities/counties
  • Electrical/Plumbing: State license required for these trades
  • Registration: Most areas require contractor registration
  • Insurance: General liability and workers' comp typically required locally

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Preliminary Notice: Not required by state law

Filing Deadline: Within 4 months of last furnishing labor or materials

Foreclosure Period: Within 6 months of project completion or last furnishing (whichever is later)

⚠️ Important: Missing lien deadlines can result in loss of payment rights. Always document your work and serve notices promptly.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 2.9% state + local rates (varies by jurisdiction)

Contractor Tax Notes: Contractors must collect sales tax on materials and taxable services
