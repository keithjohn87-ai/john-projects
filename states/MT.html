Montana Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Montana.

📋 Licensing Requirements

Montana Department of Labor and Industry

Construction Contractor Registration required

Visit DLI Website →

Key Requirements:

  • Registration: Required for construction contractors with employees
  • Exemption: Independent contractors without employees exempt (but may register)
  • Workers' Comp: Required if you have employees
  • Unemployment Insurance: May be required
  • Local Licensing: Check with city/county building departments

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Preliminary Notice: Not required for prime contractors

Filing Deadline: Within 90 days of last furnishing OR within 90 days of Notice of Completion (whichever is earlier)

Foreclosure Period: Within 2 years of filing lien

⚠️ Important: Montana has two potential deadlines - 90 days from last furnishing OR 90 days from Notice of Completion filing, whichever comes first.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: No state sales tax in Montana

Local Rates: No local sales tax

Contractor Tax Notes: Montana has no general sales tax. Contractors pay no sales tax on materials. Other business taxes may apply.
