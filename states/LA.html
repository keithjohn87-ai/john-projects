Louisiana Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Louisiana.

📋 Licensing Requirements

Louisiana State Licensing Board for Contractors (LSLBC)

Statewide licensing required for projects over $50,000

Visit LSLBC Website →

Key Requirements:

  • License Threshold: $50,000 for commercial/residential
  • Exams: Trade and business law exams required
  • Experience: Minimum experience requirements
  • Insurance: General liability required
  • Financial: Financial statement required

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Commercial Projects: Within 60 days of substantial completion or Notice of Termination

Residential Projects: Within 70 days of substantial completion

Foreclosure Period: Within 1 year of filing lien

⚠️ Important: Louisiana law changed in 2024. File a "Statement of Claim and Privilege." If Notice of Contract is filed, deadlines may vary based on contract amount.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 4.45% state rate

Local Rates: Additional local sales taxes apply (varies by parish)

Contractor Tax Notes: Contractors must collect sales tax on construction services. Materials purchased for resale are tax-exempt.
