Michigan Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Michigan.

📋 Licensing Requirements

Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs (LARA)

Bureau of Construction Codes

Visit LARA Website →

Key Requirements:

  • Residential Builder: License required for residential construction
  • Maintenance & Alteration: License for repair/remodeling
  • Pre-licensure Education: 60 hours required
  • Exam: Must pass state exam
  • Insurance: Liability insurance required

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Preliminary Notice: Not required for prime contractors

Filing Deadline: Within 90 days of last furnishing labor/materials

Foreclosure Period: Within 1 year of filing lien

⚠️ Important: File a "Claim of Lien" with the Register of Deeds in the county where property is located. The 90-day deadline is strict.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 6% state rate

Local Rates: No local sales tax in Michigan

Contractor Tax Notes: Contractors pay sales tax on materials. Construction services are generally exempt from sales tax.
