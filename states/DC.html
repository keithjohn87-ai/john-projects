District of Columbia Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Washington DC.

📋 Licensing Requirements

DC Department of Consumer and Regulatory Affairs (DCRA)

Visit DCRA Website →

Key Requirements:

  • License Required: All contractors must be licensed by DCRA
  • License Types: General Contractor, Home Improvement, Specialty
  • Bond: Surety bond required ($25,000 for general contractors)
  • Insurance: General liability and workers' compensation required

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Preliminary Notice: Not required for prime contractors

Filing Deadline: Within 90 days of last furnishing labor or materials

Foreclosure Period: Within 180 days of filing lien

⚠️ Important: A lawsuit must be filed within 180 days and a notice of pending lawsuit must be recorded within 10 days of filing suit.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 6% (no additional local tax)

Contractor Tax Notes: Contractors pay sales tax on materials. Construction labor generally not taxable. DC has specific rules for tangible personal property incorporated into real property.
