Texas Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Texas.

📋 Licensing Requirements

Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation (TDLR)

Visit TDLR Website →

Key Requirements:

  • State License: No general contractor license at state level
  • Specialty Licenses: Electrical, plumbing, HVAC regulated by TDLR
  • Local Licensing: Many cities require local contractor registration
  • Registration: Business registration with Secretary of State

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Preliminary Notice: Monthly notices required for subcontractors (2nd/3rd month notices)

Filing Deadline: By 15th day of 4th month after last furnishing (commercial) or 3rd month (residential)

Foreclosure Period: Within 1 year of filing lien (or 2 years if extended)

⚠️ Important: Texas has complex monthly notice requirements. Missing deadlines can result in loss of payment rights.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 6.25% state + up to 2% local tax

Contractor Tax Notes: New construction labor is not taxable; repair/remodeling may be taxable. Contractors pay sales tax on materials.
