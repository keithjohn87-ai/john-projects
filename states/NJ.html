New Jersey Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in New Jersey.

📋 Licensing Requirements

NJ Division of Consumer Affairs - Home Improvement Contractor Registration

Visit Registration Website →

Key Requirements:

  • Home Improvement: Registration required for work over $500
  • Public Works: Registration required for public projects
  • Trade Licenses: Electrical, plumbing, HVAC require state licensing
  • Insurance: General liability insurance required

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Residential Projects: Notice of Unpaid Balance (NUB) within 60 days; lien within 120 days

Non-Residential Projects: Lien within 90 days

Arbitration: Required for residential liens before filing

Enforcement: Within 1 year of filing

⚠️ Important: Missing lien deadlines can result in loss of payment rights. Always document your work and serve notices promptly.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 6.625%

Contractor Tax Notes: New construction is generally exempt from sales tax as a capital improvement. Contractors pay sales tax on materials purchased. Use tax applies if sales tax was not paid on out-of-state purchases.
