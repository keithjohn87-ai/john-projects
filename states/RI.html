Rhode Island Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Rhode Island.

📋 Licensing Requirements

Rhode Island Contractors' Registration and Licensing Board (RICRLB)

Visit CRLB Website →

Key Requirements:

  • Registration Required: For all residential and commercial construction work
  • Pre-License Course: 5-hour course required for residential work
  • Commercial Exemption: Commercial-only contractors exempt from course
  • Insurance: General liability insurance required
  • Registered Agent: Out-of-state contractors must have RI registered agent

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Notice of Intent: Required before filing lien

Private Projects: Within 200 days of last furnishing

Public Projects: Notice of claim within 90 days of last work

Enforcement: File lawsuit within 1 year of lien recording

⚠️ Important: Missing lien deadlines can result in loss of payment rights. Always document your work and serve notices promptly.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 7%

Contractor Tax Notes: Contractors pay sales tax on materials purchased. Nonresident contractors must have 3% of contract price withheld to secure tax payment. Use tax applies to out-of-state purchases.
