Illinois Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Illinois.

📋 Licensing Requirements

Illinois Department of Financial and Professional Regulation (IDFPR)

Division of Professional Regulation

Visit IDFPR Website →

Key Requirements:

  • State License: Illinois does not have a statewide general contractor license
  • Local Licensing: Required by most municipalities
  • Plumbing/Electrical: State license required through IDFPR
  • Roofing: State license required
  • Insurance: Varies by municipality

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Notice of Intent: Required for subcontractors - within 90 days of last furnishing

Filing Deadline: Within 4 months (120 days) of last furnishing

Foreclosure Period: Within 2 years of filing lien

⚠️ Important: Subcontractors must serve Notice of Intent to Lien on property owner within 90 days. Prime contractors have 4 months to file lien from last date of work.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 6.25% state rate

Local Rates: Additional local taxes may apply (up to 4.75%)

Contractor Tax Notes: Contractors pay sales tax on materials. Exemptions available for certain government and religious projects.
