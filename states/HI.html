Hawaii Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Hawaii.

📋 Licensing Requirements

Hawaii Contractors License Board (CLB)

Department of Commerce and Consumer Affairs (DCCA)

Visit Board Website →

Key Requirements:

  • License Types: General, Specialty, and Engineering
  • Exams: Trade and business law exams required
  • Experience: 4 years of journey-level experience
  • Bond: $5,000 contractor's bond required
  • Insurance: General liability and workers' compensation

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Preliminary Notice: Not required for prime contractors

Filing Deadline: Within 45 days after completion of work or last furnishing of labor/materials

Foreclosure Period: Within 6 months of filing lien

⚠️ Important: Hawaii requires filing an application for lien in the state circuit court. The 45-day deadline is strict and cannot be extended.

💰 Tax Information

General Excise Tax (GET): 4% state rate (Hawaii has no sales tax)

Honolulu County: 4.5% rate

Contractor Tax Notes: GET applies to all business activities including construction. Form G-37 may provide exemptions for subcontractors.
