Pennsylvania Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Pennsylvania.

📋 Licensing Requirements

PA Department of Labor & Industry

Visit DLI Website →

Key Requirements:

  • General Contractors: No state license required
  • Home Improvement: Registration required if making $5,000+ annually
  • Trade Licenses: Electrical, plumbing, HVAC require state licensing
  • Attorney General: Home improvement contractors must register with AG's office

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

General Contractors: Within 6 months of completing work

Subcontractors: Within 4 months of completing work

Notice of Filing: Serve on owner within 1 month of filing lien

Affidavit of Service: File within 20 days of serving notice

Enforcement: Within 2 years of filing

⚠️ Important: Missing lien deadlines can result in loss of payment rights. Always document your work and serve notices promptly.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 6%

Contractor Tax Notes: Contractors pay 6% sales tax on materials, equipment, and supplies used in construction. Do not charge sales tax to customers on construction contracts. Use tax applies to out-of-state purchases.
