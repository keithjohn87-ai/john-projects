Washington Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Washington.

📋 Licensing Requirements

Washington State Department of Labor & Industries (L&I)

Visit L&I Website →

Key Requirements:

  • Registration Required: All contractors must register with L&I
  • Bond: $12,000 surety bond required
  • Insurance: General liability insurance required
  • Electrical/Plumbing: Separate specialty licenses required

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Preliminary Notice: Required within 60 days of first furnishing (for subcontractors)

Filing Deadline: Within 90 days of last furnishing labor or materials

Foreclosure Period: Within 8 months of filing lien

⚠️ Important: Missing lien deadlines can result in loss of payment rights. Always document your work and serve notices promptly.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 6.5% state + local rates (varies by location)

Contractor Tax Notes: Retail sales tax applies to construction services. Prime contractors pay tax on full contract amount; subcontractors pay on their portion.
