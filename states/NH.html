New Hampshire Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in New Hampshire.

📋 Licensing Requirements

NH Office of Professional Licensure and Certification (OPLC)

Visit OPLC Website →

Key Requirements:

  • General Contractors: No state license required
  • Trade Licenses: Electrical, plumbing, HVAC require state licensing
  • Local Requirements: Check with municipal authorities
  • Asbestos/Lead: Special licensing required for abatement work

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Preliminary Notice: Not required

Notice of Intent: Must be served at least 30 days before filing lawsuit to enforce lien

Filing Deadline: Within 120 days of last furnishing labor or materials

Best Practice: File Petition within 90 days of last furnishing

⚠️ Important: Missing lien deadlines can result in loss of payment rights. Always document your work and serve notices promptly.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 0% (No state sales tax)

Contractor Tax Notes: New Hampshire does not impose a general sales tax on goods or services. However, business profits tax and business enterprise tax may apply.
