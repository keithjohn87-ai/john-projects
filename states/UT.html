Utah Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Utah.

📋 Licensing Requirements

Utah Division of Professional Licensing (DOPL)

Visit DOPL Website →

Key Requirements:

  • License Required: All contractors must be licensed
  • Classifications: Licensed by trade classification (B100, E100, R100, etc.)
  • Exams: Trade and business law exams required
  • Insurance: General liability and workers' compensation required

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Preliminary Notice: Required within 20 days of first furnishing (pre-construction notice)

Filing Deadline: Within 90 days of Notice of Completion being filed

Foreclosure Period: Within 180 days of filing lien

⚠️ Important: Utah requires a pre-construction notice for residential projects. Missing deadlines can result in loss of payment rights.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 4.85% state + local rates (average combined 7.42%)

Contractor Tax Notes: Contractors are considered consumers of materials; sales tax paid on purchases. Installation labor generally not taxable.
