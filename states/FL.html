Florida Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Florida.

📋 Licensing Requirements

Florida Department of Business & Professional Regulation (DBPR)

Visit DBPR Website →

Key Requirements:

  • License Required: For construction work over $1,000
  • Division I: General, building, residential contractors ($20,000 bond if credit < 660)
  • Division II: Specialty contractors ($10,000 bond if credit < 660)
  • Exams: Business & Finance + trade exam required
  • Insurance: General liability and workers' compensation required

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Notice of Commencement: Check before starting work

Filing Deadline: Within 90 days of last furnishing labor or materials

Foreclosure Period: Within 1 year of filing lien

Final Payment Affidavit: Required 5 days before foreclosure

⚠️ Important: Florida has strict notice requirements. Always verify Notice of Commencement before beginning work.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 6% state + local rates (varies by county)

Contractor Tax Notes: Contractors pay sales tax on materials; labor generally exempt for residential, taxable for commercial
