Maryland Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Maryland.

📋 Licensing Requirements

Maryland Home Improvement Commission (MHIC)

Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing

Visit MHIC Website →

Key Requirements:

  • License Required: For all home improvement work
  • Exam: Business and law exam required
  • Insurance: General liability required
  • Surety Bond: $20,000 bond required
  • Background Check: Required for all licensees

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Notice of Intent: Subcontractors must serve within 120 days of last furnishing

Filing Deadline: Within 180 days of last furnishing

Foreclosure Period: Within 1 year of filing lien

⚠️ Important: Maryland changed from 90 to 120 days for Notice of Intent in 1996. Prime contractors are not required to send notice before filing a lien.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 6% state rate

Local Rates: No local sales tax in Maryland

Contractor Tax Notes: Contractors pay sales tax on materials. Construction services are generally exempt from sales tax.
