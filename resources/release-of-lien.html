✅ Release of Lien

Clear the property title after payment.

What is a Release of Lien?

A Release of Lien (also called a "Lien Waiver" or "Satisfaction of Lien") is a legal document that removes a previously filed mechanics lien from a property's title. Once you receive payment, you're legally required to release the lien so the property owner can sell, refinance, or otherwise transfer the property.

Why It Matters

  • Legal requirement: Most states require lien release within a specific timeframe after payment
  • Avoid penalties: Failing to release a lien can result in fines or legal action
  • Professional reputation: Quick releases build trust with property owners and GCs
  • Clear title: Enables property transactions to proceed

Types of Lien Releases

  • Conditional Release: Effective only upon actual receipt of payment (safer for contractors)
  • Unconditional Release: Effective immediately upon signing (risky if check bounces)
  • Partial Release: Releases lien rights for a specific payment amount, not the full contract
  • Final Release: Releases all lien rights for the project upon final payment

When to Release

  • After payment clears: Wait for funds to be in your account (not just a promise)
  • Within state deadlines: Most states require release within 10-30 days of payment
  • Upon request: Property owners often require release for final payment

How to File a Release

  1. Prepare the release document — reference the original lien by date and recording number
  2. Sign and notarize — most states require notarization
  3. File with county recorder — same office where the original lien was filed
  4. Pay recording fee — typically $25-$75
  5. Send copy to property owner — proves you've released the lien

Best Practices

  • Use conditional releases until payment clears
  • Keep records of all releases filed
  • Don't sign unconditional releases before payment
  • Release promptly after payment to avoid penalties
