Connecticut Contractor Resources

Complete guide to licensing, lien laws, and compliance for contractors in Connecticut.

📋 Licensing Requirements

Connecticut Department of Consumer Protection

Visit Board Website →

Key Requirements:

  • Home Improvement Contractor license required for residential
  • Major Contractor registration for commercial
  • Pass trade exam
  • Surety bond required ($10,000-$15,000)
  • Insurance required

⚖️ Lien Law Deadlines

Preliminary Notice: Notice of Intent required for residential (subcontractors)

Filing Deadline: Within 90 days of last furnishing

Foreclosure Period: Within 1 year of filing lien

⚠️ Important: Missing lien deadlines can result in loss of payment rights. Always document your work and serve notices promptly.

💰 Tax Information

Sales Tax Rate: 6.35% state

Contractor Tax Notes: Materials taxable; labor generally exempt
