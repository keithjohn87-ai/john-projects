## Documents Created

### 1. A101-Style Contract Agreement
**File:** `construction-contract-aia.html`

A comprehensive construction contract agreement based on AIA Document A101-2017 structure for stipulated sum (fixed price) contracts.

**Key Features:**
- **Article 1:** Contract Documents (incorporates A201-2017 General Conditions by reference)
- **Article 2:** The Work (scope and project description)
- **Article 3:** Date of Commencement and Substantial Completion (schedule and liquidated damages)
- **Article 4:** Contract Sum (fixed price, alternates, allowances, Schedule of Values)
- **Article 5:** Payments (progress payments, retainage, final payment)
- **Article 6:** Completion (Substantial and Final Completion definitions)
- **Article 7:** Termination (for cause and for convenience)
- **Article 8:** Insurance and Bonds (liability, workers comp, performance/payment bonds)
- **Article 9:** Miscellaneous Provisions (governing law, notice)
- **Article 10:** Enumeration of Contract Documents

**Fillable Fields:**
- [Owner] - Name, address, contact information
- [Contractor] - Name, address, license number, contact information
- [Project] - Name, address, description
- [Contract Amount] - Stipulated sum in numbers and words
- [Dates] - Commencement, substantial completion, final completion
- [Insurance Amounts] - Liability limits, bond amounts
- [Retention Percentage] - Amount withheld from progress payments

### 2. Conditional Waiver and Release on Progress Payment (G901-Style)
**File:** `conditional-waiver-progress.html`

Used when conditionally waiving lien rights for a progress payment. The waiver is effective ONLY upon actual receipt of payment.

**Key Features:**
- Clear conditional language—waiver void if payment not received
- Project information fields
- Claimant identification (contractor, subcontractor, supplier, laborer)
- Payment details (application number, period, amount, check reference)
- Through date specification
- Exceptions preserved: future work, unpaid retention, extras, pending claims
- Notarization block (if required by jurisdiction)

**When to Use:**
- Submitting with invoice for progress payment
- When payment has not yet cleared
- When you want to ensure waiver is conditional on payment receipt

**Risk Level:** LOW—waiver is not effective until payment is received

---

### 3. Unconditional Waiver and Release on Progress Payment (G902-Style)
**File:** `unconditional-waiver-progress.html`

Used when unconditionally waiving lien rights for a progress payment. The waiver is effective IMMEDIATELY upon signing.

**Key Features:**
- Prominent warning about unconditional nature
- Verification of payment receipt and clearing
- Project and claimant information
- Payment details with form of payment received
- Through date specification
- Exceptions preserved: future work, unpaid retention, extras, pending claims
- Notarization block

**When to Use:**
- After payment has been received and cleared in your account
- When payor requires immediate waiver effectiveness
- When you are certain payment is good

**Risk Level:** MEDIUM—waiver is effective immediately even if payment later fails

---

### 4. Conditional Waiver and Release on Final Payment (G903-Style)
**File:** `conditional-waiver-final.html`

Used for the FINAL PAYMENT on a project. Covers ALL Work through Final Completion. The waiver is effective ONLY upon actual receipt of final payment.

**Key Features:**
- Final payment designation
- Complete contract summary (original sum, change orders, total, previous payments, retention)
- Conditional language—waiver void if final payment not received
- Complete waiver of ALL lien rights for the Project upon payment
- Affidavit of Payment of Debts and Claims included
- Exceptions: warranty claims, pending excluded claims, indemnification
- Notarization block

**When to Use:**
- Submitting for final payment
- When final payment has not yet cleared
- When you want to ensure waiver is conditional on final payment receipt

**Risk Level:** MEDIUM—covers all Work but is conditional on payment

---

### 5. Unconditional Waiver and Release on Final Payment (G904-Style)
**File:** `unconditional-waiver-final.html`

Used for the FINAL PAYMENT on a project. Covers ALL Work through Final Completion. The waiver is effective IMMEDIATELY upon signing.

**Key Features:**
- Critical warning about unconditional nature
- Verification of final payment receipt and clearing
- Complete contract summary
- Complete and total waiver of ALL lien rights for the Project
- Affidavit of Payment of Debts and Claims included
- Exceptions: warranty claims, pending excluded claims, indemnification
- Notarization block

**When to Use:**
- After final payment has been received and cleared in your account
- When payor requires immediate final waiver effectiveness
- When you are certain final payment is good

**Risk Level:** HIGH—waives ALL rights immediately and irrevocably

## Usage Guidelines

### For the A101-Style Contract:
1. Fill in all bracketed fields with project-specific information
2. Attach required documents (drawings, specifications, A201 General Conditions)
3. Review insurance and bond requirements
4. Have both parties sign and date
5. Distribute executed copies to all parties

### For Lien Waivers:
1. **Choose the right form:**
   - Progress payment + payment not yet received = G901 (Conditional Progress)
   - Progress payment + payment received = G902 (Unconditional Progress)
   - Final payment + payment not yet received = G903 (Conditional Final)
   - Final payment + payment received = G904 (Unconditional Final)

2. **Fill in completely:**
   - Project information
   - Claimant information
   - Payment details
   - Through date (critical!)

3. **Verify before signing:**
   - Payment amount is correct
   - Through date covers only Work paid
   - Exceptions are understood

4. **Execute properly:**
   - Sign and date
   - Have notarized if required
   - Keep a copy for your records

## Important Considerations

### State-Specific Requirements
- Many states have statutory lien waiver forms that MUST be used
- Some states prohibit certain types of waivers
- Notarization requirements vary by jurisdiction
- Pre-lien notice requirements vary by state

**Always check your state's lien laws before using these forms.**

### AIA Document References
These templates are based on the following official AIA documents:
- **A101-2017:** Standard Form of Agreement Between Owner and Contractor (Stipulated Sum)
- **A201-2017:** General Conditions of the Contract for Construction
- **G901-2022:** Conditional Waiver and Release on Progress Payment
- **G902-2022:** Unconditional Waiver and Release on Progress Payment
- **G903-2022:** Conditional Waiver and Release on Final Payment
- **G904-2022:** Unconditional Waiver and Release on Final Payment

### When to Consult an Attorney
- Before using any contract for the first time
- When modifying standard language
- If your state has specific requirements
- For projects over $100,000
- For complex or unusual project structures
- If you don't understand a provision
