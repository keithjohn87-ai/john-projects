# QUICK START GUIDE
## TradeElegance Alabama Steel Erection Package

### Welcome
This package gives you everything you need to estimate, contract, and manage steel erection projects in Alabama. Follow this guide to get started in 30 minutes.

---

## STEP 1: SETUP (10 minutes)

### A. Install the Estimating Spreadsheet
1. Open `estimating/alabama-steel-estimator.csv` in Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets
2. **Save As** `My-Company-Name-Estimator.xlsx` (keep the original as backup)
3. Add your company information:
   - Company name
   - Alabama license number
   - Contact information

### B. Update Labor Rates
Call your insurance agent and get:
- Current workers compensation rate (per $100 of payroll)
- General liability insurance cost percentage
- Update the rates in Sheet 3 (Labor Rates)

### C. Customize Material Costs
Contact your steel suppliers and update:
- Current beam prices (per linear foot)
- Connection hardware costs
- Delivery charges for your area

---

## STEP 2: TEST WITH A REAL PROJECT (15 minutes)

Pick a past project you know the costs on:

1. Open the Materials sheet (Sheet 2)
2. Enter quantities from your old project
3. Check if the calculated total matches what you actually spent
4. Adjust markup percentage if needed

**Tip:** If your actual costs were higher, increase the contingency percentage from 3% to 5%.

---

## STEP 3: CREATE YOUR FIRST PROPOSAL (5 minutes)

1. Open `templates/proposal-template.txt`
2. Copy the content into Microsoft Word or Google Docs
3. Replace all `[bracketed]` sections with your info
4. Fill in pricing from the estimating spreadsheet
5. Save as PDF and send to client

## ALABAMA-SPECIFIC NOTES

### Licensing
- Required: Alabama Home Builders License or appropriate classification
- Verify license at: https://www.alhomebuilders.org/

### Taxes
- Alabama state sales tax: 4%
- Local taxes vary by county (1-3%)
- Total typically 5-7% for materials

### Workers Compensation
- Steel erection class code: 5040
- Rates vary by experience modifier
- Contact: Alabama Department of Labor (334) 956-7450

### Permits
- Required in all Alabama jurisdictions
- Fees typically 1-2% of construction value
- Contact local building department early

## COMMON QUESTIONS

**Q: Can I use this for other states?**  
A: The estimating works anywhere, but the contract language is Alabama-specific. Check local laws before using in other states.

**Q: What if my actual costs are different?**  
A: Update the spreadsheet after each job. Track your variances to improve future estimates.

**Q: Do I need an attorney to review the contract?**  
A: Recommended for your first few uses. After that, if you're comfortable with the terms, it's your call.

**Q: Can I share this with my crew?**  
A: Yes, for internal use. Do not distribute to other contractors without purchasing additional licenses.
